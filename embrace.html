"Embrace". 2019. Acrylic on canvas. 36 x 9 in.

<-- -->

"Embrace" is a surrealistic painting of a woman with her arms spread out in surrender and her face tilted upwards, whilist four butterflies are perched on various parts of her body.

I started creating this piece when I was very frustrated, angry, and emotionally drained. I allowed by emotions to flow onto the canvas through creating a colorful, distorted, and abstract background, which is meant to resemble a turbulent and stormy sky. From there, I got the idea to add the woman, seeing her embracing the darkness and frustration in her heart and mind and allowing it to consume and embrace her. I lastly added the butterflies and a lot of glitter to add to the composition and overall vibe of the piece.

I feel many times, we try to bottle up our emotions and neglect them. We act as if we are always fine and dandy when we truly are not. With "Embrace", I want the explain how we must find some medium to express our emotions- whether it be frustration, sadness, happiness, etc. When we embrace our emotions, we are able to grow and welcome a calm and lightness that we deprived ourselves of when we bottle up our feelings.

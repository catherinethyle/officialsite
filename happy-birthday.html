"Happy Birthday". 2020. Acrylic on wooden and glass. 32.5 x 18.5 in (8.25 x 6.25 in. glass pieces)

<-- -->

For my 18th birthday, I wanted to create a "sef portrait" of sorts. Seeing how we has an old wood and glass panel just laying outside, I decided this was a sign to experiment with painting on wood and glass. The six glass panels show Vietnam, my face, a hand with flowers floating above it, a human heart, a cat standing in front of the moon, and feet dipped in a shallow body of water. These six glass panels are surrounded by clouds. All these panels are representational of who I am. I am a Vietnamese American. This is how my face looks close-up. Sometimes I clench my fist when I'm frustrated or nervous. I have a kind heart. Some people call me cat and I was born at midnight. I enjoy basking in the beauty and calmness nature provides us. My head and mind are at times far away, floating around in the vast sky above us.s

At this time, I started to realize how much I enjoyed painting in a style with rough brush strokes, something about the texture and strong pressense it had was so alluring to me. With my color palette, I wanted to use pastels, softer colors to balance out the boldness of the brush strokes. For my first time working with glass and wood, I thik this piece turned out quite well. The whole process from ideation to execution took some time but I ended p finishing everything on my birthday, May 23rd, hence the name of this body of work being "Happy Birthday".

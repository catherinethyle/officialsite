"Motherland". 2020. SublimeText & Adobe Photoshop.

<-- -->

"Motherland" is a glitch art piece of 10+ images of Vietnam. Many people think of the War when they first think of Vietnam. With this piece, I want to show that Vietnam is more than a country where a war was fought. It is a beautiful country and the home of my parents. Vietnam is a land of beautiful beaches, high skyscrapers, pretty skies, and rich culture. This collage captures some of the beauty that is present in Vietnam in a colorful and playful manner, while still recognizing the pain, sadness, and scars left from the past (i.e. the Vietnam War and poverty). Through this collage, I want to show that the “ugly”, though part of the country and its history, does not define it. That poverty left in the wake of war does not equate to ugliness and despair. That hope and beauty can exist at the “top” and at the “bottom” of the social ladder. The Vietnamese people, especially those from the South or Center that were greatly affected by the war, are still able to live happily and embrace their culture and traditions, whether they are still living in Vietnam or have found refuge elsewhere.

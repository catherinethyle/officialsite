"Love". 2021. Wax paper, wooden skewers, & found objects. 10 1/4 x 9 3/4 x 3 in.



Love. It can be found in mundane objects, relics, and memories. We all have our own love languages and this piece shows some of mine. As a horder of many things that I see hold sentimental value, this piece is a capsule of some of the items that I love and I feel radiate what love is. The wooden skewers and wax paper together are meant to act as a foggy window, as if you're looking at someone's forgotten memories of the past or into a dusty window of an abandoned building. With this piece, I want to show how there is love in the little things around us, that we usually forget about until it is too late.

This piece was my first attempt at sculpture. I was heavily inspired by Yuji Agematsu's collection of "zips" and Doris Salcedo's "Abatarios". Both of their works include found objects displayed in opaque or translucent containers, which I excecuted with my own twist.

"The Me". 2020. Adobe Photoshop.

<-- -->

With "The Me", I wanted to showcase how the person and individual that you act as in front of others does not always refect the true you. Many times, we bring forth a fake alter identifty- whether it be in real life or through social media. The mask of not showing your true self will begin to surround and proceed to swallow you up until you have become engulfed and burried in this fake persona. "The Me" is a short motion graphic that shows what a person presents does not always reflect the inner working of their mind, feelings, experiences, or life. One can seem to live a beautiful and happy life on the outside but actually be a broken and battered mess on the inside. This piece serves as a remind to be kind to others because you never know what someone is battling internally. This also applies to being kind to ourselves and not invalidating our own feelings and experiences. We should all take care of our mental and physical states and learn to recognize and accept the emotions we feel.

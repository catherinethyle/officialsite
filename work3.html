"Motherland". 2020. SublimeText & Adobe Photoshop.


This is a glitch art piece of 10+ images of Vietnam. Many people think of the War when they first think of Vietnam. With this piece, I wanted to show that Vietnam is more than a country where a war was fought. It is a beautiful country and the home of my parents. Vietnam is a land of beautiful beaches, high skyscrapers, pretty skies, and rich culture. This collage captures some of the beauty that is present in Vietnam in a vibrant and playful manner, which still recognizing the pain and sadness of its past (i.e. the Vietnam War and poverty). Through this collage, I want to show that the “ugly”, though part of the country and its history, does not define it. That poverty and war does not equate to ugliness and despair. That hope and beauty can exist at the “top” and at the “bottom” of the social ladder. The Vietnamese people, especially those in the South and Center that were greatly affected by the war, are still able to live happily and embrace their culture and traditions, whether they are still living in Vietnam or have found refuge elsewhere.

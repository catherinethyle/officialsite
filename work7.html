"Untitled Site Art Prototype". 2021. Blender 3D. 15 x 15 ft.


Growing up, I remember always seeing Vietnamese grandmas at church wearing jade bracelets and pearl earrings with their áo dài (Vietnamese traditional dresses) on special holidays. But these two gemstones are not limited to the jewlery boxes of older Vietnamese women, but many Vietnamese individuals. Jade, pearl, and cherry blossoms are some things that are seen as beautiful in Vietnamese culture. With this piece, I wanted to pay homage to my Vietnamese roots and Vietnamese culture.

As mentioned before, I first thought of Vietnamese grandmas, which led to thinking of Asian elders and the recent attacks on individuals in the AAPI community. I know the elderly often enjoy going on walks and getting their fresh air and now feel unsafe to do so with the Anti-Asian sentiment revolving because of the COVID-19 virus. With that in mind, I wanted to create a site art piece that would be in a park for individuals to rest, located under a cherry blosson tree. There are 8 pearls in the three because eight is a lucky number in Vietnamese culture.

If this protoype is able to become a tangible thing in the future, I would like it to be installed in a park in an area heavily populated by elderly Vietnamese individuals and others in the AAPI community. I imagine making it so that the jade and pearls light up in the evening to illuminate the cherry blossom tree and provide a beautiful sight to see at night and a tranquil space to take shelter from the sun's rays during the day.

This piece was heavily inspired the work of Beverly Buchanan, specifically her "March Ruins" piece (concrete and found elements. 1979). Buchanan's work was understated in the fact that it blended in with the site it was placed in. The slabs of concrete and tabby looked like rocks that were meant to be in that location, where people could just sit and rest on. Though this piece is a bit more flamboyant and not as "natural" looking as Buchanan's work in the sense that it seems like it was something that would be on site, I created it so it looks as if it is a type of circular bench one can sit on and rest at.

Though I had some prior experience with Blender 3D, I decided to be more ambitious and play around more with the software with this piece. I was able to expand my knowledge of using Blender's keyframe freatures and nodes with the help of YouTube tutorials and clicking around whie creating. Though there were some problems along the way, I was able to work around them and learn some new skills.
